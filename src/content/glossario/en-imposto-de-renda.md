## What it is
Income tax is a mandatory levy that all Brazilians must pay annually if their income exceeds a certain [value](/en/glossario/en-valor-financeiro). It is calculated based on the taxpayer’s annual gross income, which includes salaries, pensions, rentals, [interest](/en/glossario/en-juros) and other types of earnings. The amount of income tax varies according to the taxpayer’s income bracket, with higher earners paying a larger percentage of their salary.

* The exemption bracket is R$ 1.903,98 for those who earn up to R$ 1.903,98 per month  
* The rate is 7.5% for those who earn between R$ 1.903,99 and R$ 2.826,65 per month  
* The rate is 15% for those who earn between R$ 2.826,66 and R$ 3.751,05 per month  
* The rate is 22.5% for those who earn between R$ 3.751,06 and R$ 4.898,19 per month  
* The rate is 27.5% for those who earn above R$ 4.898,19 per month

Income tax is an important tool for the government to raise resources and fund public programs and services, such as health, education, and security.

## How it works
Income tax is calculated based on the annual adjustment declaration, which must be filed by the taxpayer by the end of April each year. In this declaration, the taxpayer must report all of their income and expenses from the previous year, including salaries, pensions, rentals, interest and other types of earnings. The tax amount is then computed from the information provided.

**Practical tip:** Keep all income and expense receipts from the previous year—they’ll be needed when you file your annual adjustment declaration.

In addition, remember that income tax can be paid in up to 8 installments, as long as the taxpayer files the annual adjustment declaration by the end of April. If the taxpayer fails to pay the income tax, they will be subject to fines and interest.

## Advantages
Income tax has several advantages, including:
* Funding public programs and services, such as health, education and security  
* Reducing income inequality, because higher earners pay a larger percentage of their salary  
* Stimulating the economy, as the government can use the collected resources to invest in infrastructure and other projects  

Moreover, income tax can also be used as a fiscal policy tool to encourage or discourage certain economic activities.

## [Risks](/en/posts/en-cdb-vs-tesouro-selic-qual-rende-mais-em-2026)
However, income tax also carries some risks, including:
* Higher tax burden, which can affect families’ consumption capacity  
* Complexity of the annual adjustment declaration, which can lead to errors and fines  
* Risk of evasion, which can result in lost revenue for the government  

**Practical tip:** It’s wise to hire an accountant or a specialized professional to help you with the annual adjustment declaration, especially if you have a complex income situation.

Also, keep in mind that income tax is a legal obligation, and evasion can lead to serious consequences, including fines and even imprisonment.

## Practical examples
Let’s look at a few practical examples of how income tax works:
* João earns R$ 5.000 per month and has an annual gross income of R$ 60.000. He pays 15% of his salary in income tax, which amounts to R$ 9.000 per year.  
* Maria earns R$ 3.000 per month and has an annual gross income of R$ 36.000. She pays 7.5% of her salary in income tax, which amounts to R$ 2.700 per year.  
* Pedro earns R$ 8.000 per month and has an annual gross income of R$ 96.000. He pays 27.5% of his salary in income tax, which amounts to R$ 26.400 per year.  

**Practical tip:** Remember that income tax can be deducted from your salary, which can lower your tax burden. Also, consider deducting medical and educational expenses from your tax return.
