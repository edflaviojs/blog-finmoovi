## What is CDB?
The CDB is an investment that allows investors to lend money to a bank or financial institution for a specified period, receiving a fixed return in exchange. It's a popular option for those looking to invest in fixed income, as it offers a higher yield than a savings account, but with a slightly higher risk.

## How does CDB work?
When you invest in a CDB, you're essentially lending money to the bank for a specified period, which can range from a few days to several years. In return, the bank pays a fixed return on the invested amount, calculated based on the interest rate established at the time of investment.

For example, if you invest $4,500 (approximately R$ 10,000,00) in a CDB with an interest rate of 10% per year, you'll receive $450 in returns at the end of the year, totaling $4,950.

## Types of CDB
There are different types of CDB, each with its own characteristics and risks. Some of the main types include:

| Type of CDB | Characteristics |
| --- | --- |
| CDB with daily liquidity | Allows withdrawal of the investment at any time, with returns proportional to the investment time |
| CDB with monthly liquidity | Allows withdrawal of the investment monthly, with fixed returns |
| CDB with fixed term | Requires the investment to be held for a specified period, with fixed returns |

## Practical examples
Suppose you want to invest $2,250 (approximately R$ 5,000,00) in a CDB with an interest rate of 12% per year, with daily liquidity. If you withdraw the investment after 6 months, you'll receive $2,730 (approximately R$ 5,600,00), which is $2,250 (approximately R$ 5,000,00) plus $480 (approximately R$ 600,00) in returns.

If you invest $4,500 (approximately R$ 10,000,00) in a CDB with an interest rate of 10% per year, with a fixed term of 2 years, you'll receive $5,445 (approximately R$ 12,100,00) at the end of the term, which is $4,500 (approximately R$ 10,000,00) plus $945 (approximately R$ 2,100,00) in returns.
