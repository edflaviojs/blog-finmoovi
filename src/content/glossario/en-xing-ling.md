## What it is

Xing ling is a collective investment method that combines the simplicity of a **fixed‑income** fund [/en/glossario/en-renda-fixa] with the flexibility of a micro‑investment app. Instead of buying an entire security, you buy “pieces” of a portfolio that is already diversified in government bonds, CDBs (Certificates of Deposit) and debentures of solid companies. Each “ling” represents a fraction of **R$1,000** of the portfolio, and you can acquire as many as you want, according to the money you have available. The goal is to offer predictable returns, but higher than **[savings](/en/glossario/en-poupanca)**, without requiring advanced market knowledge.

![What it is](/images/glossario/xing-ling-inline-1.webp)

- **Investment piece:** each ling is worth **R$1,000**, but can be purchased in fractions of **R$100**.  
- **[Automatic diversification](/en/glossario/en-diversificacao):** the fund already contains different assets, reducing risk.  
- **Daily liquidity:** you can redeem your money at any time, like in a checking account.

## How it works

The operation of xing ling follows three simple steps: you deposit money in the app, choose how many ling you want to buy and the system allocates your contribution among the assets that make up the portfolio. The return calculation is done daily, based on the interest rate of the securities that compose the fund. When you decide to withdraw the money, the app sells the corresponding fraction of the assets and credits the amount to your account within up to 24 hours.

- **Deposit:** transfer from **R$100** to **R$5,000** via TED or PIX.  
- **Allocation:** the algorithm distributes your contribution among short‑ and medium‑term securities.  
- **Return:** the gross rate (before taxes) usually stays between **6 % and 8 %** per year.  
- **Redemption:** request the withdrawal and the amount reaches your bank account in one business day.

## Advantages

For people earning between **R$3,000** and **R$8,000**, xing ling brings benefits that go beyond simply saving money. First, the return rate is usually two to three times higher than savings, which pays about **2 %** per year. Second, automatic diversification protects against the drop of a single asset. Finally, the practicality of the app lets you monitor the balance and returns in real time, as if it were a finance game.

![Advantages](/images/glossario/xing-ling-inline-3.webp)

- **Superior return:** **6 %‑8 %** per year versus **2 %** from savings.  
- **Low entry amount:** you can start with **R$100**, ideal for tight budgets.  
- **Automatic management:** no need to pick securities, the algorithm does everything.  
- **Access via mobile:** total control in the palm of your hand, like a friend who helps you organize the money.

**Practical tip:** **Start with a contribution of [R$500](/en/posts/en-montando-carteira-diversificada-com-r-500) and increase gradually as your budget allows.**  

## [Risks](/en/posts/en-cdb-vs-tesouro-selic-qual-rende-mais-em-2026)

Although xing ling is safer than individual stocks, it is not risk‑free. The main risk is interest‑rate variation: if the Central Bank raises the rate, the securities already bought may lose market value, although the return is still guaranteed until maturity. Another point of attention is management fees, which can reduce your net gain if the fund is very small. Finally, because the investment depends on an app, there is a risk of technical or cybersecurity failures.

- **Interest‑rate risk:** changes can affect the value of the securities.  
- **Management fees:** normally between **0.5 % and 1 %** per year, impact the final profit.  
- **Technological risk:** vulnerabilities in the app can compromise data.  
- **Limited liquidity:** although daily, very large redemptions can lead to small loss of return.

**Practical tip:** **Always check the management fee before investing; prefer [funds](/en/glossario/en-fundo-de-investimento) with costs below **0.8 %** per year.**  

## Practical examples

Let's imagine three monthly‑income profiles and how xing ling can be used to improve financial health:

- **João, salary **R$3,200**:** he decides to allocate 10 % of his salary (**R$320**) to xing ling. In 12 months, with a 7 % annual return, he will have approximately **R$3,440**, earning **R$140** in interest.  
- **Mariana, salary **R$5,500**:** she sets aside **R$550** (10 % again) and increases the contribution to **R$1,100** each quarter. After a year, the balance reaches about **R$6,200**, generating **R$700** profit.  
- **Carlos, salary **R$8,000**:** he invests **R$800** per month and, every six months, adds an extra contribution of **R$2,000**. In 12 months, total invested is **R$13,600** and the return reaches **R$15,200**, resulting in **R$1,600** gain.

These numbers show that, even with modest contributions, the effect of compound interest (interest on interest) can turn small savings into a significant cushion over time.

**Practical tip:** **Automate the monthly contribution via automatic debit; that way you don’t forget to invest.**  

## How to start

If you’re ready to put xing ling into practice, follow these simple steps:

- **Download the app:** search “Xing Ling” in the app store and install.  
- **Create your account:** fill in name, CPF and e‑mail; verification takes a few minutes.  
- **Define your initial contribution:** choose between **R$100**, **R$500** or **R$1,000**, according to what fits your budget.  
- **Select the frequency:** set up automatic monthly contribution or make one‑off deposits when you have spare money.  
- **Monitor the returns:** open the app daily and see the balance grow; adjust the contribution if you want to speed up the process.
