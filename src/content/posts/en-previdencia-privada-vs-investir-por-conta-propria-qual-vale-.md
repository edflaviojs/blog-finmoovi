## Introduction

When it comes to [financial planning](/en/posts/5-dicas-para-usar-as-ferias-para-reorganizar-suas-financas), one of the main concerns is ensuring a good retirement. Two popular options for achieving this goal are [private pension](/en/glossario/en-previdencia-privada) and investing on your own. However, many people wonder: which is the best choice? In this article, we will explore the pros and cons of both options, creating a comparative table to help you decide.

## How Private Pension Works

Private pension is a long-term [investment](/en/posts/tesouro-direto-para-iniciantes) plan that offers a monthly income after retirement. These plans are offered by financial institutions and insurance companies, and can be customized according to the investor's needs and objectives. Private pension can be done through plans such as VGBL (Vida Gerador de Benefício Livre) or PGBL (Plano Gerador de Benefício Livre).

![How Private Pension Works](/images/posts/previdencia-privada-vs-investir-por-conta-propria-qual-vale--2.webp)



An example of how private pension works: imagine that you invest R$ 500 per month for 20 years in a private pension plan with an interest rate of 8% per year. At the end of this period, you will have invested around R$ 120,000, and you can receive a monthly income of approximately R$ 2,500 after retirement.

## Comparative Table

| Criterion | Private Pension | Investing on Your Own |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Objective** | Monthly income after retirement | Accumulation of patrimony |

| **Return** | Fixed or variable returns, depending on the plan | Variable returns, depending on the performance of investments |
| **[Liquidity](/en/glossario/en-liquidez)** | Low liquidity, as resources are blocked until retirement | High liquidity, as you can sell investments at any time |

## When to Choose Private Pension

Private pension is a good option for you if:

* You are looking for a guaranteed monthly income after retirement;
* You have no experience in investments and prefer that a professional manage your resources;
* You are willing to block your resources for a long period of time in exchange for a guaranteed monthly income.

## When to Choose Investing on Your Own

Investing on your own is a good option for you if:

![When to Choose Investing on Your Own](/images/posts/previdencia-privada-vs-investir-por-conta-propria-qual-vale--6.webp)



* You have experience in investments and are willing to manage your own resources;
* You are looking for more flexibility and control over your investments;
* You are willing to take on more risk in search of a higher return.

## Verdict

In summary, the choice between private pension and investing on your own depends on your financial profile and objectives. If you are looking for a guaranteed monthly income and have no experience in investments, private pension may be the best option. On the other hand, if you have experience in investments and are looking for more flexibility and control, investing on your own may be the best choice.
