## Introduction to Spreadsheets and [Financial Apps](/en/posts/voce-tambem-tem-dificuldade-em-organizar-suas-financas)
If you're one of the people who use spreadsheets to manage your finances, you're not alone. Many of us started using spreadsheets because of their flexibility, free access, and customization. However, as time passes, we may realize that the spreadsheet is no longer sufficient to meet our financial needs. In this article, we'll explore how migrating from a spreadsheet to a financial app can be a good option for you.

## Advantages of Spreadsheets
Before we talk about financial apps, it's essential to recognize the advantages of spreadsheets. They are:
* Flexible: you can create customized columns and rows to meet your specific needs.
* Free: most spreadsheets are free, which is a significant benefit for those who are starting to manage their finances.
* Customizable: you can personalize your spreadsheet to include only the information that's important to you.

However, despite these advantages, spreadsheets can have some disadvantages. For example, they can be:
* Difficult to keep updated: it's easy to forget to update your spreadsheet regularly, which can lead to errors and inconsistencies.
* Limited in terms of mobility: if you don't have access to a computer, it can be challenging to access and update your spreadsheet.

## When a Financial App Makes More Sense
A financial app can be a good option for you if:
* You need consistency: financial apps can help you keep your finances updated and consistent, even when you're busy.
* You need mobility: financial apps allow you to access and update your finances from anywhere, at any time.
* You need automation: financial apps can automate financial tasks, such as bill payments and [money](/en/posts/30-day-rule-for-purchases) transfers.
* You need alerts: financial apps can provide alerts and notifications to help you stay on track.

According to research, 70% of people who use spreadsheets give up within 30 days due to lack of consistency. This happens because spreadsheets can be difficult to keep updated and consistent, especially if you have a busy life.

## Checklist: Spreadsheet or App?
Here's a checklist to help you decide if a spreadsheet or a financial app is better for you:
* **Spreadsheet is for you if:**
 + You like to customize your finances and create personalized columns and rows.
 + You're comfortable with the idea of manually updating your finances.
 + You don't need mobility or automation in your finances.
* **App is for you if:**
 + You need consistency and mobility in your finances.
 + You want to automate financial tasks, such as bill payments and [money](/en/posts/creating-an-emergency-fund) transfers.
 + You need alerts and notifications to stay on track.
