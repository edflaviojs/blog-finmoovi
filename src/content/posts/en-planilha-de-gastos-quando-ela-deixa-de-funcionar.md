## Introduction to Expense Spreadsheets
Expense spreadsheets are a powerful tool for managing your [personal finances](/en/posts/5-dicas-para-usar-as-ferias-para-reorganizar-suas-financas). They offer flexibility, are free, and can be customized to meet your needs. With a [spreadsheet](/en/posts/migrando-da-planilha-para-um-app-financeiro), you can record all your [expenses](/en/posts/metodo-50-30-20-para-salario-brasileiro), create a [budget](/en/posts/50-30-20-rule-complete-guide), and track your financial situation in detail. However, despite all these advantages, many people give up using expense spreadsheets after a short period.

## Advantages and Disadvantages of Expense Spreadsheets
Expense spreadsheets have several advantages, such as:
- **Flexibility**: You can create customized columns and rows to meet your specific needs.
- **Free**: Most expense spreadsheets are free, making them accessible to everyone.
- **Customizable**: You can adapt the spreadsheet to fit your lifestyle and spending habits.

However, research shows that 70% of people give up using expense spreadsheets within 30 days due to lack of consistency. This happens because keeping an expense spreadsheet up-to-date requires discipline and time. Additionally, it's easy to forget to record some [expenses](/en/posts/5-dicas-para-se-preparar-financeiramente-para-o-segundo-seme) or not have access to the spreadsheet when needed.

## Checklist: Spreadsheet or App?
Here's a checklist to help you decide if a spreadsheet or an app is more suitable for you:
- **Spreadsheet is for you if...**
  - You like to have total control over how your expenses are recorded and categorized.
  - You have a simple lifestyle and few expenses to record.
  - You're not comfortable using apps or technology.
- **App is for you if...**
  - You have an active lifestyle and many expenses to record.
  - You need reminders and notifications to keep your expenses organized.
  - You want to have access to detailed reports and analyses of your expenses and finances.
