## Investing Is Not the Same as Saving  

When we talk about “saving [money](/en/posts/30-day-rule-for-purchases),” the first image that usually pops up is a savings account or that piggy bank under the mattress. But saving and investing are two different, though complementary, strategies.  

- **Saving** is simply setting aside part of your income to use later, without expecting a significant return. It’s the first step toward financial security.  
- **Investing** means putting that money into assets that can earn more than [inflation](/en/glossario/en-inflacao), taking on some risk to chase growth.  

The difference lies in the goal and the timeline. If the goal is to pay the electricity bill next month, saving makes sense. If the goal is to secure a comfortable retirement or buy a house in 10 years, investing is the smarter choice.

## How Savings Accounts Work  

Savings still have their place, especially for people with low risk tolerance. In Brazil, the return on a savings account follows the rule of the Reference Rate (TR) + 0.5% per month when the Selic rate is above 8.5% per year. In practice, this usually yields between 0.3% and 0.5% per month, barely covering inflation.  

![Como funciona a poupança  ](/images/posts/diferenca-entre-investir-e-poupar-o-que-realmente-importa-2.webp)

Imagine you earn R$4,500 a month and manage to set aside 10% (R$450) every month. After a year, with the savings account yielding 0.4% per month, you would have roughly R$5,590. If annual inflation is 4%, your purchasing power has basically not changed.  

The advantage of a savings account is liquidity: the money is available at any time, without bureaucracy. But the opportunity cost – what you miss out on by not investing in higher‑yield options – can be high.

## Types of Investments for Those Earning Between R$3,000 and R$8,000  

| Income profile | Recommended strategy | Why it works |
|----------------|----------------------|--------------|
| R$3,000 to R$5,000 | Treasury Direct (Tesouro Selic) | Low risk, higher return than savings, daily liquidity |
| R$5,001 to R$8,000 | Fixed‑income or multi‑market funds | Diversification, possibility of gains above Tesouro Selic |
| R$8,000+ | Stocks and ETFs (e.g., BOVA11) | Long‑term appreciation potential, but requires more monitoring |

**Practical example:** João earns R$6,000 and decides to allocate 15% (R$900) to invest. He puts R$600 into Tesouro Selic (average return of 0.6% per month) and R$300 into a fixed‑income fund that yields 0.8% per month. In 12 months, Tesouro will be worth about R$7,300 and the fund about R$3,660, totaling R$10,960 – far more than what a savings account would generate with the same contribution.

If you still have doubts, it’s worth checking the official sites of the [Central Bank of Brazil](https://www.bcb.gov.br) and [Tesouro Direto](https://www.tesourodireto.com.br), where you’ll find simulators and detailed explanations.

## Difference Over the Time Horizon  

The main reason investing outperforms a savings account is time. The longer the period, the greater the effect of [compound interest](/en/glossario/en-juros-compostos).  

![Diferença no horizonte de tempo  ](/images/posts/diferenca-entre-investir-e-poupar-o-que-realmente-importa-4.webp)

- **Short term (up to 12 months):** a savings account can be enough if the priority is safety and quick access.  
- **Medium term (1 to 5 years):** Tesouro Selic or fixed‑income funds already start to make a difference.  
- **Long term (over 5 years):** stocks, ETFs, and multi‑market funds come into play, as [volatility](/en/glossario/en-volatilidade) smooths out and return potential rises.  

If you aim to buy a car in three years, a mix of Tesouro Selic + fixed‑income funds could be the ideal combination. For retirement, which is 30 years away, allocating to equities gives a higher chance of building a comfortable nest egg.

## Practical Tip  
**Practical tip:** Build a “safety belt” of 3 to 6 net salaries in a high‑liquidity account (like Tesouro Selic). Once you reach that amount, direct the excess to investments that match your horizon and risk tolerance. This separation prevents you from having to touch your investments in emergencies and still lets the money work for you.

## Start Today  

Don’t wait for the next sale or the end of the month to put theory into practice. Choose an amount that fits your budget, open an account with a trustworthy brokerage, and make your first contribution. Every cent you don’t invest is a cent less of potential growth.
