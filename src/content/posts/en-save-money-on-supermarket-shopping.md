## [Planning](/en/posts/orcamento-com-salario-minimo-dicas-praticas) is Key
When we go to the supermarket, it's common to buy more than we need, especially if we don't have a clear plan of what we want to buy. This can lead to excessive spending and food waste. To avoid this, it's essential to make a shopping list before going to the supermarket. For example, if you spend around $500 per week on supermarket shopping for a family of four, you can start reducing that amount by identifying the essential items and those that can be cut.

A practical example is the case of a person who earns $5,000 per month and spends around $1,500 on dining out. If that person starts cooking at home and planning their shopping, they can easily reduce that amount to $1,000, [saving](/en/posts/criar-reserva-de-emergencia) $500 per month.

## Organize Your Shopping List
A well-organized shopping list can help avoid impulse purchases and ensure that you only buy what you need. Here are some tips to organize your shopping list:
* **Practical tip:** Make a weekly shopping list and divide it into categories, such as fruits, vegetables, meats, dairy products, etc.
* Check what you already have at home before going to the supermarket to avoid duplicate purchases.
* Plan your meals for the week and make a shopping list based on that.

## Choose the Right Supermarket
The choice of supermarket can also affect your spending. Some supermarkets offer lower prices than others, especially for basic items like rice, beans, and pasta. It's essential to compare prices among supermarkets in your area and choose the one that offers the best prices.

![Organize your shopping list](/images/posts/economize-nas-compras-do-supermercado-2.webp)



For example, if you spend $100 per week at one supermarket and discover that another supermarket in the area offers the same items for $80, you can save $20 per week, which is $1,040 per year.

## Use Coupons and Promotions
Many supermarkets offer coupons and promotions to help reduce spending. It's essential to check the supermarkets' websites and social media to find these offers. Additionally, many supermarkets have loyalty programs that can help you save even more.

An example is the case of a person who spends $200 per week at a supermarket and discovers that the supermarket offers a 10% discount coupon for purchases over $150. If that person buys $200 per week, they can save $20 per week, which is $1,040 per year.

![Use coupons and promotions](/images/posts/economize-nas-compras-do-supermercado-4.webp)



## Track Your [Expenses](/en/posts/metodo-50-30-20-para-salario-brasileiro)
Tracking your expenses is crucial to understanding where your [money](/en/posts/30-day-rule-for-purchases) is going and how you can save. It's essential to record all your expenses, including supermarket shopping, and check if you're within your [budget](/en/posts/50-30-20-rule-complete-guide).

**Practical tip:** Use a expense tracking app to record your expenses and track your [budget](/en/posts/5-dicas-para-reorganizar-suas-financas-nas-ferias). This can help identify areas where you can cut back on spending and save.

## Start Today
You don't need to wait until next month to start saving on supermarket shopping. You can start today by making a shopping list and planning your meals for the week. Remember that small changes can lead to significant [savings](/en/posts/financial-education-for-children) over time.
